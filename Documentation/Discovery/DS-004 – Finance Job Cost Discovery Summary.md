# DS-004 – Finance / Job Cost Discovery Summary

# Document ID: DS-004
# Version: 0.1
# Status: Complete

# Purpose

This discovery workshop examined how Job Cost supports financial management, operational decision-making, forecasting, and continuous improvement throughout the project lifecycle.

# Key Business Discoveries
**Financial Data Validation**

    Job Cost validates operational and accounting information before financial reporting is finalized.

**Labor as a Leading Indicator**

    Labor performance is the largest controllable factor affecting project profitability and frequently influences burden and equipment costs.

**Forecasting Over Historical Reporting**

    Management places greater value on forecasting future project outcomes than simply reviewing historical financial information.

**Continuous Budget Management**

    Budgets should be updated as soon as estimating errors, field conditions, or approved scope changes become known.

**Closed Job Reviews**

    Completed projects provide valuable operational and estimating feedback that supports continuous improvement.

**Progressive Investigation**

    Financial investigations begin with overall project performance and progressively drill into phases, cost codes, cost types, and transactions.

**Executive Performance Monitoring**

    Weekly executive reporting combines actual performance, historical performance, and projected future performance within a single management scorecard.

**Role-Based Reporting**

    The same underlying information should be filtered differently depending on organizational responsibility.

**Business Impact**

    The Construction Analytics Solution should emphasize proactive financial management, early identification of project issues, and continuous organizational learning through integrated operational and financial analytics.
